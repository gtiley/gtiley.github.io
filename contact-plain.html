About me

I am a biologist broadly interested in molecular evolution. This includes the theoretical aspects of phylogenetics and population genetics, and its applications to biodiversity and conservation science. I use molecular tools in various plant groups, primates, and birds to develop multiple working policy alternatives for land management, ecological resilience, and public health.

I am passionate about education in genetics, computing, and mathematics. I aim to provide early scientists the tools to innovate and develop creative solutions to for biological challenges. At the same time, I do not lose sight of the importance of being naturalists connected to empirical systems and valuing collections and databases.

I want to push for tighter integration of science, economic policy, and labor. Basic research has historically provided material benefits to the working class. I believe land management policy can leverage untapped climate financing to put money back into communities most hurt by biodiversity loss. All basic research, educational, and policy goals strive to increase equity in science at both domestic and international levels.
