About me

I am a biologist broadly interested in molecular evolution. This includes the theoretical aspects of phylogenetics and population genetics, and its applications to biodiversity and conservation science. My research also aims to understand variation in rates of molecular evolution among species and along genomes.

I am passionate about education in genetics, computing, and mathematics. We can develop contemporary education programs that incorporate strong fundamentals from these fields while still allowing creative exploration at the individual level. Critical thinking skills can be developed while also setting concrete goals for technical skill-building.

I want to push for tighter integration of science and labor. Basic research has historically led to benefits that materially benefit working families. Science can be a force for good of the working class and be more inclusive. This means cutting across race, gender, and class boundaries through targeted consistent pressure in key areas. There is untapped potential for making conservation science revenue generating and forming meaningful industry partnerships. I am happy to discuss specific actions with others that want to increase the appeal and influence of the scientific community.
